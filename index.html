Research Iteams

Decline of Agricultural Land and Wetlands in Dhaka’s Detailed Area Plan (DAP) 2022-2035: A Critical Analysis of RAJUK’s Proposed Land Use

This study analyzes the reduction of agricultural land and wetlands in Dhaka’s Detailed Area Plan (DAP) 2022-2035. While current data shows 124,372.49 acres of agricultural land and 52,804.5 acres of wetlands, RAJUK’s proposed plan allocates only 107,797.41 acres and 29,593.5 acres, respectively—a significant decline inconsistent with recent studies. The findings highlight urgent concerns about ecological sustainability and food security, urging revisions to align the DAP with empirical land cover evidence.

Environmental and Anthropogenic Factors Influencing the Distribution and Abundance of Hilsa (Tenualosa Ilisha) of the Andharmanik River, Bangladesh

This study examines environmental and anthropogenic factors affecting hilsa (Tenualosa ilisha) distribution in Bangladesh’s Andharmanik River. Key findings show that pH and turbidity significantly influence hilsa abundance, while anthropogenic threats like overfishing, small-mesh nets, dredging, brickfield pollution, and sedimentation further reduce populations. The study stresses the need for long-term, large-scale monitoring to ensure sustainable hilsa yields.

Light Pollution: An Emerging Pollution Issue

Light pollution, a growing environmental issue caused by excessive artificial lighting, disrupts ecosystems and human health while wasting energy. Despite its benefits (extended productivity/recreation), uncontrolled night lighting harms wildlife, plants, and energy sustainability. Global initiatives now promote modified lighting practices to balance development with environmental protection.

End-of-life potential of photovoltaic (PV) cell waste in Bangladesh

This study evaluates the end-of-life potential of photovoltaic (PV) cell waste in Bangladesh using Material Flow Analysis (MFA). By analyzing historical solar installation data (2005–2024), the research estimates PV waste generation, material composition (glass, aluminum, plastic, copper, silicon), and projects cumulative waste up to 2050. A Facebook Prophet model forecasts future installations (2025–2030), validated with performance metrics (RMSE, MAE, MAPE, R²). The study also assesses the economic value of recovered materials and conducts a cost-benefit analysis for recycling, alongside policy reviews to address PV waste management challenges.

Genetically Modified Crops in Agriculture: Balancing Promises, Risks, and Regulatory Challenges for Sustainable Food Security

This review examines the dual narrative of genetically modified (GM) crops in modern agriculture, from their revolutionary potential to their contentious risks. Since the advent of DNA recombination technology in 1973, GM crops—such as herbicide-resistant soybeans and insect-resistant maize—have addressed critical challenges like yield enhancement, pest resistance, and nutritional fortification. However, their rapid adoption raises concerns over biodiversity loss, superweeds, antibiotic resistance, and human health impacts (e.g., allergies). By synthesizing current debates, this study highlights the urgent need for stringent regulations and precautionary measures to ensure GM crops' safety while harnessing their benefits for global food security.
